Historicity of Pétion-Ville and Delmas

Pétion-Ville, a suburb of Port-au-Prince, Haiti, is situated in the hills east of the city on the northern hills of the Massif de la Selle. Established in 1831 by President Jean-Pierre Boyer, it was named after Alexandre Sabès Pétion (1770–1818), a Haitian general, and president, acknowledged as one of the country's four founding fathers..✅✅✅Delmas, a municipality in the Port-au-Prince metropolitan area, holds a unique history and significance. Originally part of the French colonial estate Grande Colline, it became part of independent Haiti after gaining freedom from France in 1804. Throughout the 19th century, Delmas witnessed substantial development and urbanization.
