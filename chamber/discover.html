Historicity of Pétion-Ville and Delmas

Pétion-Ville is a commune and a suburb of Port-au-Prince, Haiti, in the hills east and separate from the city itself on the northern hills of the Massif de la Selle. Founded in 1831 by president Jean-Pierre Boyer, it was named after Alexandre Sabès Pétion (1770–1818), the Haitian general and president later recognized as one of the country's four founding fathers.✅✅✅ Delmas is another municipality within the Port-au-Prince metropolitan area with a unique history and significance. Colonial and Post-Independence Era: Delmas was originally part of the French colonial estate known as the Grande Colline. After Haiti gained its independence from France in 1804, it became part of the independent nation. During the 19th century, it saw significant development and urbanization.Growth and Development: Over the years, Delmas has experienced significant urban growth, becoming a key residential and commercial area. It is known for its bustling markets, businesses, and cultural life. It has also been the site of various historical and cultural events.🌀 🐬 🌀🐬 🌀 read more
